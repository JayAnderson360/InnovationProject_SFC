Our Conservation Mission

At Sarawak Forestry Corporation, conservation is at the heart of everything we do. Our mission is to preserve and protect Sarawak's rich biodiversity through sustainable practices, research, education, and community engagement.

Sarawak is home to some of the world's most diverse ecosystems, including ancient rainforests, unique coastal areas, and intricate river systems. These environments support countless species of flora and fauna, many of which are endemic to Borneo and found nowhere else on Earth.

Our conservation efforts are guided by scientific research, local knowledge, and international best practices. We work tirelessly to balance the protection of our natural heritage with sustainable development, ensuring that Sarawak's incredible biodiversity will continue to thrive for generations to come.

Through our dedicated conservation programs, we focus on habitat preservation, species protection, ecosystem restoration, and raising public awareness about the importance of biodiversity conservation.

Conservation Efforts & Projects

Key initiatives that are making a difference for Sarawak's biodiversity

Orangutan Rehabilitation

Our orangutan rehabilitation program focuses on rescuing, rehabilitating, and releasing orphaned or displaced orangutans back into protected forest habitats. We also conduct research on orangutan behavior and ecology to inform conservation strategies.

Rare Plant Conservation

Our plant conservation initiatives focus on protecting Sarawak's endangered flora species, including rare orchids, pitcher plants, and endemic trees. We conduct botanical surveys, maintain seed banks, and restore threatened plant habitats throughout protected areas.

Forest Restoration

Our reforestation initiatives focus on restoring degraded forest areas, creating wildlife corridors, and enhancing habitat connectivity. We plant native tree species and monitor forest recovery over time.

Anti-Poaching Efforts

We work with law enforcement to combat wildlife poaching and illegal logging. Our ranger teams conduct regular patrols in protected areas, gather intelligence, and monitor wildlife populations.

Community-Based Conservation

We partner with local communities to develop sustainable livelihoods that reduce pressure on natural resources. These initiatives include ecotourism, sustainable agriculture, and handicraft production.

Research & Monitoring

Our scientific research programs study biodiversity, ecosystem health, and wildlife populations. This research informs conservation strategies and helps us measure the impact of our efforts.

Visitor Guidelines: Do's & Don'ts

Help us protect Sarawak's biodiversity by following these important guidelines

Do's

  • Stay on designated trails and paths to minimize habitat disturbance

  • Observe wildlife from a safe and respectful distance

  • Take all trash with you and dispose of it properly

  • Support local conservation efforts through responsible tourism

  • Report any suspicious activities or wildlife sightings to park rangers

  • Learn about the local ecosystem before your visit

  • Respect local cultures and traditions

Don'ts

  • Feed, touch, or disturb wildlife in any way

  • Remove any plants, animals, or natural objects from the environment

  • Use single-use plastics in natural areas

  • Make loud noises that could disturb wildlife

  • Light fires outside of designated areas

  • Purchase products made from endangered species

  • Enter restricted or sensitive habitat areas

Totally Protected Wildlife

These endangered species are fully protected by law in Sarawak

Sarawak's totally protected wildlife species are protected under the Wildlife Protection Ordinance. It is illegal to hunt, kill, capture, sell, or keep these animals without proper authorization.

Penalties for violations include a maximum fine of RM50,000 and up to 5 years imprisonment.

Protected Wildlife

These vulnerable species receive protection under Sarawak's wildlife laws

Protected wildlife species in Sarawak have regulations that allow limited hunting or possession only with a license from the Controller of Wildlife.

Violations can result in imprisonment for 1 year and a fine of RM10,000.

Totally Protected and Protected Plants

Sarawak's remarkable plant species that are protected by conservation laws

Sarawak's plant conservation laws protect various endemic and endangered plant species. Totally protected plants carry a penalty of RM25,000 and 2 years imprisonment, while protected plants have penalties of RM10,000 and 1 year imprisonment.

Violations can result in imprisonment for 2 years and a fine of RM25,000.

It is prohibited to collect, store, plant, burn, poison or sell these protected plants without a license from the Controller of Wildlife, Sarawak.
